Featured Books

To Kill a Mockingbird
by Harper Lee

"To Kill a Mockingbird" (1960) follows Scout Finch as she navigates the complexities of race and morality in Depression-era Alabama. Through Atticus Finch, a lawyer defending an innocent black man, the novel explores themes of injustice and the loss of innocence. "To Kill a Mockingbird" is a timeless story that challenges us to confront difficult truths about society.
